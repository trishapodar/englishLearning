SECTION A — 1 Mark Each

Q1.   Horizontal and vertical axes   [1 Mark]

Answer: The horizontal line is the x-axis, and the vertical line is the y-axis.

Marking: 0.5 marks for each correct name. Accept: x-axis and y-axis. Common Error: Confusing the two lines or simply writing "axes" without specifying.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

Q2.   Point of intersection   [1 Mark]

Answer: Origin.

Marking: 1 mark for correct name. Accept: Origin or (0,0). Common Error: Center.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

Q3.   Position of (0, -4)   [1 Mark]

Answer: The point lies on the negative y-axis.

Marking: 1 mark for stating y-axis. (Specifying negative y-axis is perfectly fine). Accept: y-axis. Common Error: Stating a quadrant (like 3rd or 4th quadrant) since points with x=0 lie directly on the y-axis, not inside a quadrant.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

Q4.   Coordinates from distance   [1 Mark]

Answer: (-7, -5).

Marking: 1 mark for correct coordinates with correct signs. Accept: P(-7, -5). Common Error: Writing (-5, -7). Remember, distance from the y-axis gives the x-coordinate magnitude (7), and from the x-axis gives the y-coordinate magnitude (5). Third quadrant means both are negative.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

Q5.   Signs in second quadrant   [1 Mark]

Answer: Abscissa is negative (-), and ordinate is positive (+).

Marking: 1 mark for correct signs. Accept: (-, +). Common Error: Reversing the signs (+, -) which is for the fourth quadrant.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

SECTION B — 2 Marks Each

Q6.   Coordinates on axes   [2 Marks]

Answer: Point on the x-axis at a distance of 4 units to the right of origin is (4, 0). Point on the y-axis at a distance of 3 units below the origin is (0, -3).

→ Identifying point on right x-axis: (4, 0) — 1 mark.

→ Identifying point on lower y-axis: (0, -3) — 1 mark.

Marking: 1 mark for each correct pair of coordinates. Accept: Correct ordered pairs. Common Error: Reversing the components, e.g., (0, 4) or (-3, 0).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

Q7. ★   Foot of perpendiculars   [2 Marks]

Answer: B is the foot of the perpendicular on the x-axis, so its y-coordinate is 0. Its coordinates are (3, 0). C is the foot of the perpendicular on the y-axis, so its x-coordinate is 0. Its coordinates are (0, 4).

→ Correct coordinates of B (3, 0) — 1 mark.

→ Correct coordinates of C (0, 4) — 1 mark.

Marking: 1 mark for B, 1 mark for C. Accept: Plotting the points and deriving coordinates graphically. Common Error: Writing B as (4,0) and C as (0,3).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

SECTION C — 3 Marks Each

Q8.   Finding the 4th vertex of a square   [3 Marks]

Answer: The length of side PQ on the x-axis is from x=1 to x=4, which is 3 units. Similarly, PS is from y=0 to y=3, which is 3 units. Since it's a square, R must be 3 units to the right of S and 3 units above Q. The x-coordinate of R will match Q (4) and the y-coordinate will match S (3).

→ Plotting the three points P(1,0), Q(4,0), S(1,3) correctly on graph — 1.5 marks.

→ Identifying that PQ = PS = 3 units — 0.5 marks.

→ Writing coordinates of R as (4, 3) — 1 mark.

Marking: Plotting (1.5), side length logic/visual completion (0.5), R coordinate (1). Accept: Reading R directly from graph. Common Error: Naming vertices out of order (PQRS).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

Q9. ★   Finding rectangle vertex and area   [3 Marks]

Answer: D must have the same x-coordinate as A (x=2) and the same y-coordinate as C (y=-1). Therefore, D is (2, -1).

Length of the rectangle = Distance between x=2 and x=-3 = 5 units.
Width of the rectangle = Distance between y=2 and y=-1 = 3 units.
Area = Length × Width = 5 × 3 = 15 sq units.

→ Correct plotting of points — 1 mark.

→ Correct coordinates of D(2, -1) — 1 mark.

→ Correct calculation of length (5), width (3) and area (15 sq units) — 1 mark.

Marking: Plotting (1), D coordinate (1), Area (1). Accept: Reading side lengths directly from graph. Common Error: Miscalculating length by doing 2 - 3 instead of 2 - (-3).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3

SECTION D — 5 Marks Each

Q10.   Triangle plotting and area calculation   [5 Marks]

Answer:

→ (a) Plot the points A(0,0), B(5,0), and C(5,4) and join them. Since B is on the x-axis and the line BC (from x=5, y=0 to x=5, y=4) is perpendicular to the x-axis (A to B), the angle at B is 90 degrees. Therefore, ABC is a right-angled triangle. (2 marks: 1.5 for plot, 0.5 for naming triangle)

→ (b) Length of AB (along x-axis) = 5 - 0 = 5 units. Length of BC (along a vertical line x=5) = 4 - 0 = 4 units. (1.5 marks)

→ (c) Area of right triangle ABC = ½ × base × height = ½ × AB × BC = ½ × 5 × 4 = 10 sq units. (1.5 marks)

Marking: Plotting graph accurately (1.5), Right-angled triangle (0.5), Lengths (0.5+1=1.5), Area formula and result (1.5). Accept: Area calculation by box-counting on graph paper. Common Error: Naming the triangle as scalene without noting the 90-degree angle.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 3
