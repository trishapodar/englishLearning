Q1.   Discovery of living cells   [1 Mark]

Q2.   Amoeba's food engulfing process   [1 Mark]

Answer: Endocytosis (or phagocytosis).

Marking: Correct term — 1 mark. Accept: Phagocytosis. Common Error: Writing diffusion or osmosis — these are passive processes, not engulfing.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q3.   RBC in hypertonic solution   [1 Mark]

Answer: It will shrink / undergo crenation due to exosmosis (loss of water to the hypertonic surroundings).

Marking: Stating it will shrink — 1 mark. Accept: Lose water and shrink / crenation / plasmolysis. Common Error: Stating it will burst — bursting occurs in hypotonic solutions.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q4.   Prokaryotic genetic material location   [1 Mark]

Answer: Nucleoid.

Marking: Correct term — 1 mark. Accept: Nucleoid region. Common Error: Writing "nucleus" — prokaryotes do not have a membrane-bound nucleus.

SECTION B — 2 Marks Each

Q5.   Cell Theory postulates   [2 Marks]

Answer: (i) All living organisms are composed of cells and cell products. (ii) The cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life. (iii) All cells arise from pre-existing cells (Virchow's addition). Proposed by Schleiden and Schwann (1838–39).

→ Any two valid postulates — 1 mark each.

Marking: First postulate (1 mark) + Second postulate (1 mark). Names of scientists not required. Accept: Any two correctly stated postulates from the Cell Theory. Common Error: Writing only one postulate and expecting 2 marks.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q6.   Selectively permeable membrane   [2 Marks]

Answer: The plasma membrane allows only selected materials to enter or exit the cell (it is selective about what it permits through), while blocking the movement of others. This selectivity helps the cell maintain its internal environment.

→ Explaining it allows specific substances (1 mark) + blocks others / maintains internal balance (1 mark).

Marking: Selective permeability explained (1 mark) + restricting unwanted substances (1 mark). Accept: Semi-permeable membrane explanation. Common Error: Just saying "it allows things to pass" without emphasising the selectivity.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q7.   Diffusion vs osmosis   [2 Marks]

Answer: Diffusion is the movement of any substance (solid, liquid, or gas) from a region of higher concentration to lower concentration and does not require a membrane. Osmosis is the movement of water only, from a region of lower solute concentration to higher solute concentration, through a semi-permeable membrane.

→ Diffusion definition / key feature (1 mark) + Osmosis definition / key feature (1 mark).

Marking: Correct definition or key difference for diffusion (1 mark) + for osmosis (1 mark). Accept: "Osmosis requires a semi-permeable membrane; diffusion does not." Common Error: Confusing the concentration gradient of solute versus solvent.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q8. ★   Salt on leaf peel — plasmolysis   [2 Marks]

Answer: The concentrated salt solution is hypertonic relative to the leaf cells. Water moves out of the cells into the salt solution by exosmosis. As a result, the cell contents (cytoplasm) shrink away from the cell wall — a process called plasmolysis.

→ Identifying exosmosis / water leaving cell (1 mark) + stating shrinkage / plasmolysis (1 mark).

Marking: Exosmosis/water exit (1 mark) + plasmolysis/shrinkage (1 mark). Accept: Valid equivalent description of cell contraction. Common Error: Stating the cells will swell or burst.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

→ Example 1: Nerve cells (neurons) — long and branched to transmit impulses over long distances (1 mark).

Q10.   Plasma membrane composition   [3 Marks]

Answer: The plasma membrane is made of a phospholipid bilayer with proteins embedded within and on its surface. Its fluid nature (fluidity) allows the cell membrane to be flexible — enabling the cell to engulf food and other materials from outside through endocytosis, and allowing membrane proteins to move freely.

→ Phospholipid bilayer (1 mark) + embedded proteins (1 mark) + how fluidity helps (endocytosis/flexibility) (1 mark).

Marking: Lipid bilayer (1 mark) + proteins (1 mark) + role of fluid nature (1 mark). Accept: Reference to the fluid mosaic model. Common Error: Listing cellulose as a component — cellulose is for cell walls, not membranes.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q11. ★   Raisin experiment — osmosis   [3 Marks]

Answer: Step 1 — In plain water (hypotonic solution), raisins have a higher solute concentration than the surrounding water. Water enters the raisins by endosmosis → they swell. (1.5 marks)
Step 2 — In concentrated sugar syrup (hypertonic solution), the syrup has a higher solute concentration than the raisins. Water exits the raisins by exosmosis → they shrink back. (1.5 marks)

Marking: Endosmosis and swelling (1.5 marks) + exosmosis and shrinking (1.5 marks). Accept: Correctly identifying hypotonic and hypertonic environments with direction of water movement. Common Error: Using the term "diffusion" instead of osmosis.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q12.   Active vs passive transport   [3 Marks]

Answer: Passive transport moves substances along the concentration gradient (high to low) without using energy. Active transport moves substances against the concentration gradient (low to high) using energy in the form of ATP.

→ Passive transport — no energy, along gradient (1 mark).

→ Active transport — uses energy (ATP), against gradient (1 mark).

→ Example: Absorption of minerals by root hair cells from soil (1 mark).

Marking: Passive (1 mark) + Active (1 mark) + example (1 mark). Accept: Sodium-potassium pump as an alternate example. Common Error: Forgetting to provide an example or confusing the direction of transport.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

SECTION D — 5 Marks Each

Q15.   Nucleus structure and role   [5 Marks]

Answer (a) — Structure [3 marks]:

→ The nucleus is surrounded by a double-layered nuclear membrane (nuclear envelope) with nuclear pores for exchange of material. (1 mark)

→ Inside is the nucleoplasm which contains chromatin material (DNA + protein). Before cell division, chromatin condenses into chromosomes. (1 mark)

→ A small, dense body called the nucleolus is present inside the nucleus; it synthesises RNA and ribosomes. (1 mark)

Answer (b) — Role [2 marks]:

→ Role in reproduction: The nucleus directs cell division by replicating its DNA and distributing chromosomes to daughter cells. (1 mark)

→ Role in activities: The nucleus acts as the control centre of the cell — the DNA contains genes that direct all metabolic and chemical activities through protein synthesis. (1 mark)

Marking: Structure — 3 marks (nuclear membrane + chromatin + nucleolus). Role — 2 marks (reproduction + control). Accept: Mention of nuclear pores, genes, or protein synthesis. Common Error: Missing the distinction between chromatin (interphase) and chromosomes (division phase).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5

Q16.   Endocytosis/Exocytosis + egg experiment   [5 Marks]

Answer (a) — Definitions [2 marks]:

→ Endocytosis: The process by which the cell engulfs external material by inward folding of the plasma membrane, forming a vesicle. (1 mark)

→ Exocytosis: The reverse process — vesicles inside the cell fuse with the plasma membrane and expel their contents to the outside. (1 mark)

Answer (b) — Egg observations [3 marks]:

→ In pure water (hypotonic): water moves into the egg by endosmosis (osmosis inward) since the egg has a higher solute concentration → egg swells. (1.5 marks)

→ In concentrated salt solution (hypertonic): the salt solution has a higher solute concentration than the egg → water exits the egg by exosmosis → egg shrinks. (1.5 marks)

Marking: Endocytosis definition (1 mark) + Exocytosis definition (1 mark) + Egg in water (1.5 marks) + Egg in salt (1.5 marks). Accept: Correct direction of water movement with correct scientific terms. Common Error: Confusing endocytosis/exocytosis with osmosis; not naming the osmotic process for the egg observations.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 5
