SECTION A — 1 Mark(s) Each

Q1.   800 cm   [1 Mark(s)]

1 m = 100 cm. So, 8 m = 8 × 100 = 800 cm.

Marking: 1 mark for the correct answer.
Accept: 800
Common Error: Writing 80 cm.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Mathematics, Ch. 2, Ex. 2.1

Q2.   Liter / Litre   [1 Mark(s)]

Liter (L)

gram (g)

SECTION B — 2 Mark(s) Each

Q5.   7 m 75 cm   [2 Mark(s)]

Length of first ribbon = 5 m 30 cm

Length of second ribbon = 2 m 45 cm

Total Length = (5+2) m and (30+45) cm = 7 m 75 cm.

Marking: 1 mark for column addition setup. 1 mark for correct final answer.
Accept: 7.75 m
Common Error: Forgetting to carry over correctly if cm goes above 100.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Mathematics, Ch. 2, Ex. 2.2

Q6.   4 L 450 mL   [2 Mark(s)]

6 L 800 mL - 2 L 350 mL

Liters: 6 - 2 = 4 L

Milliliters: 800 - 350 = 450 mL

Result: 4 L 450 mL

Marking: 1 mark for correct ml subtraction. 1 mark for correct L subtraction.
Accept: 4450 mL
Common Error: Borrowing incorrectly between L and mL.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Mathematics, Ch. 7, Ex. 7.2

SECTION C — 3 Mark(s) Each

Q7.   7 kg 500 g   [3 Mark(s)]

Weight of potatoes = 4 kg 250 g

Weight of onions = 2 kg 500 g

Weight of tomatoes = 0 kg 750 g

Total g = 250 + 500 + 750 = 1500 g

1500 g = 1 kg 500 g

Total kg = 4 + 2 + 0 + 1 (carried) = 7 kg

Total Weight = 7 kg 500 g

Marking: 1 mark for correct vertical alignment. 1 mark for finding sum of grams (1500g). 1 mark for carrying over 1kg and correct final answer.
Accept: 7.5 kg
Common Error: Failing to convert 1500 g into 1 kg and 500 g.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Mathematics, Ch. 8, Ex. 8.3

Q8.   500 m   [3 Mark(s)]

Length (L) = 150 m

Width (W) = 100 m

Boundary / Perimeter = 2 × (L + W)

Perimeter = 2 × (150 + 100) = 2 × 250 m = 500 m

Marking: 1 mark for correct formula or logic. 1 mark for sum of L+W. 1 mark for final multiplication and unit.
Accept: 150+150+100+100 = 500m
Common Error: Adding just 150+100=250 m.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Mathematics, Ch. 13, Ex. 13.1

SECTION D — 5 Mark(s) Each

Q9.   (a) 10 m 45 cm, (b) 14 m 55 cm   [5 Mark(s)]

Part (a): Total cloth used

Cloth for shirt = 5 m 60 cm

Cloth for trousers = 4 m 85 cm

Total used = 5 m + 4 m and 60 cm + 85 cm

145 cm = 1 m 45 cm

Total used = 9 m + 1 m 45 cm = 10 m 45 cm.

Part (b): Cloth left

Total initial cloth = 25 m 00 cm

Cloth left = 25 m 00 cm - 10 m 45 cm

Borrow 1 m: 24 m 100 cm - 10 m 45 cm = 14 m 55 cm.

Marking: 2 marks for correctly adding the used cloth in part (a). 1 mark for correctly borrowing 1m as 100cm in part (b). 2 marks for correctly subtracting to find the leftover cloth.
Accept: 10.45 m and 14.55 m
Common Error: Subtracting 45 cm from 00 cm by ignoring the borrowing step from meters.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Mathematics, Ch. 2, Ex. 2.4
