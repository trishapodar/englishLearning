Constitutional & Legal Developments

FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS · HIGH IMPORTANCE

Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Amendment Bill, 2026

The Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment introduced the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Amendment Bill, 2026, proposing significant changes to the 2019 Act. Most controversially, the Bill removes the right to self-perceived gender identity and replaces it with certification by a Medical Board.

  • Original Act (2019): Allowed self-identification of gender
  • NALSA v. Union of India (2014): SC recognised "third gender" and upheld self-determination under Articles 14, 15, 19 & 21
  • New Bill: Introduces mandatory Medical Board for identity certification — critics call this "coercive medicalisation"
  • Constitutional tension: Bill may override a Supreme Court landmark judgment
CLAT Angle: This topic directly links to Articles 14, 15, 19 & 21. Understanding NALSA (2014) and constitutional morality is essential. CLAT often tests comprehension of legal reasoning behind such controversies.
SUPREME COURT · SEPARATION OF POWERS

CAPF Bill vs Supreme Court: A Constitutional Tug-of-War

The Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) Bill, 2026 attempts to negate a May 2025 Supreme Court judgment directing the Home Ministry to progressively reduce IPS officer deputation in CAPFs to Inspector-General rank. Parliament's move to override a SC ruling has sparked a constitutional debate on the separation of powers.

  • SC Judgment (May 2025): Directed reduction of IPS deputation in paramilitary forces
  • CAPF Bill: Asserts IPS officers are "necessary" for Union-State coordination
  • Constitutional concern: Legislative override of judicial pronouncements
CLAT Angle: Tests knowledge of separation of powers doctrine, judicial review, and the relationship between Parliament and the judiciary.
SUPREME COURT · DISABILITY RIGHTS

SC Stresses Accessibility for Persons with Disabilities in Transport

The Supreme Court directed modifications to cab services to accommodate wheelchairs and other assistive devices, emphasizing first-mile and last-mile connectivity for persons with disabilities. The ruling is expected to create new compliance standards for cab aggregators and transportation companies.

  • Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 mandates accessibility
  • Article 21: Right to dignity and a barrier-free environment
  • India ratified the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD)
CLAT Angle: A strong topic connecting constitutional rights, statutory law, and international obligations — all themes CLAT examiners favour.
SUPREME COURT · RTI

Delhi HC: PM CARES Fund Retains RTI Privacy Protection

The Delhi High Court observed that the PM CARES Fund retains a right to privacy under the Right to Information Act, even if it is assumed to be a public authority. The ruling reignites the ongoing debate about transparency and accountability of the fund created during COVID-19.

  • PM CARES Fund set up in March 2020 during COVID-19
  • Key issue: Whether PM CARES qualifies as a "public authority" under RTI Act, 2005
  • RTI Act: Balances transparency with privacy under Section 8 exemptions
CLAT Angle: RTI Act, judicial review, and the right to information vs. right to privacy (Article 21) are recurring CLAT themes.
GOVERNORS · CONSTITUTIONAL CONTROVERSY

Governors' Walkout During Legislative Sessions: Constitutional Debate

Governors of Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Kerala recently walked out during inaugural State Legislative Assembly sessions in opposition-ruled states, triggering a fresh debate on the scope of gubernatorial discretion under the Constitution.

  • Article 153: Governor as constitutional head of the State
  • Article 161-163: Discretionary powers of Governors
  • Sarkaria Commission & Punchhi Commission addressed Centre-State relations
  • SC has held: Governor must act on aid and advice of Council of Ministers in most matters
CLAT Angle: Centre-State relations and the role of the Governor are classic CLAT topics under constitutional law.

Education, Policy & Governance

EDUCATION · JUDICIARY

SC Stays UGC Equity Regulations 2026

The Supreme Court of India stayed the implementation of the University Grants Commission's (UGC) Equity Regulations 2026, which had sparked widespread opposition from universities and faculty groups. The stay halts a controversial new framework pending further judicial scrutiny.

  • UGC: Statutory body under UGC Act, 1956 regulating higher education
  • SC has power of judicial review under Articles 32 and 226
  • The case raises questions about autonomy of educational institutions
CLAT Angle: Education governance, UGC's role, and the Supreme Court's power of judicial review are important topics.
LEGISLATION · EXAM INTEGRITY

Chhattisgarh Enacts Tough Anti-Cheating Law

The Chhattisgarh Assembly enacted legislation imposing up to 10 years imprisonment and fines up to ₹1 crore for specified violations in public examinations. The law follows a nationwide push for stringent anti-paper-leak legislation after high-profile exam fraud cases.

  • Centre enacted the Public Examinations (Prevention of Unfair Means) Act, 2024
  • State legislatures can enact concurrent/supplemental laws on education
  • Education is in the Concurrent List (List III, Schedule 7)
CLAT Angle: Legislative competence, Concurrent List, and criminal law amendments are tested regularly in the legal reasoning section.

International Affairs

GEOPOLITICS · CRITICAL

🔴 Iran-Israel Conflict Escalates: Operation Epic Fury

A joint US-Israeli airstrike on February 28, 2026 destroyed Iran's Supreme Leader's compound, killing Ayatollah Khamenei. Iran launched retaliatory strikes on Gulf states hosting US bases. The Strait of Hormuz has become a flashpoint, causing a global oil price surge and flight disruptions across Gulf hubs. The conflict poses serious implications for India's energy security, trade routes, and the Indian diaspora in the Gulf.

CLAT Angle: India's energy dependence on Gulf, impact on the Indian diaspora (8.9 million in GCC), Strait of Hormuz as a chokepoint, and international law of armed conflict.
GLOBAL WELFARE

🌍 World Happiness Report 2026

The World Happiness Report 2026 ranked Finland as the happiest country for the ninth consecutive year, with Afghanistan at the bottom. The report flagged the negative impact of excessive social media use on youth wellbeing globally. Rankings are based on GDP per capita, social support, life expectancy, freedom, generosity, and corruption perception.

CLAT Angle: International reports like the World Happiness Report, HDI, and Press Freedom Index are a staple of GK passages in CLAT.

Environment & Science

ENVIRONMENT · JUDICIARY

SC Allows Removal of 45,000+ Mangroves for Versova-Bhayander Road Project

The Supreme Court upheld a Bombay High Court order permitting the Brihanmumbai Municipal Corporation (BMC) to remove over 45,000 mangroves for the Versova-Bhayander coastal road project. The verdict pits development imperatives against environmental protection obligations.

  • Mangroves are protected under the Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) Notification
  • Environment Protection Act, 1986: Broad central government powers to protect environment
  • Article 48A (DPSP): State shall endeavour to protect the environment
  • Article 51A(g): Fundamental Duty to protect the natural environment
CLAT Angle: Environmental law, DPSPs, Fundamental Duties and the tension between development and ecology are high-frequency CLAT themes.
WATER · CONSTITUTION

SC Orders Tribunal for Tamil Nadu-Karnataka Pennaiyar River Dispute

The Supreme Court directed the Union government to constitute a tribunal to adjudicate the long-pending inter-state water dispute between Tamil Nadu and Karnataka over the Pennaiyar River.

  • Article 262: Parliament may by law provide for adjudication of disputes related to inter-state rivers
  • Inter-State River Water Disputes Act, 1956 governs tribunal formation
  • Cauvery, Krishna, and now Pennaiyar are key inter-state river disputes
CLAT Angle: Article 262 and inter-state river disputes are classic CLAT constitutional law questions.

🎯 Weekly CLAT Practice Quiz

Click "Reveal Answer" to check each answer after attempting.

Q1. Which landmark Supreme Court judgment (2014) upheld the right to self-identification of gender identity for transgender persons?
NALSA v. Union of India (2014) — The SC recognised transgender persons as the "third gender" and held that self-determination of gender identity is a fundamental right under Articles 14, 15, 19, and 21.
Q2. Under which Article of the Indian Constitution can Parliament legislate for adjudication of inter-state river water disputes?
Article 262 — Parliament may by law provide for adjudication of disputes relating to waters of inter-state rivers or river valleys, and may exclude the jurisdiction of the Supreme Court or other courts in such matters.
Q3. Finland topped the World Happiness Report 2026. Which of the six indicators is NOT part of the happiness score calculation? (a) Corruption Perception (b) Military Expenditure (c) Social Support (d) Generosity
(b) Military Expenditure. The six indicators are: GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity, and perceptions of corruption.
Q4. Education is listed under which schedule and list of the Indian Constitution?
Seventh Schedule, List III (Concurrent List), Entry 25. Both Parliament and State Legislatures can make laws on education, but in case of conflict, Parliamentary law prevails (Article 254).
Q5. The Strait of Hormuz, a flashpoint in the Iran-Israel conflict, connects which two water bodies?
The Strait of Hormuz connects the Persian Gulf with the Gulf of Oman (and the Arabian Sea). About 20% of the world's oil passes through this chokepoint, making it strategically critical for global energy security.
