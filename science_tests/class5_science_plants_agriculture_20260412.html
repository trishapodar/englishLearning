SECTION B — 2 Marks Each

Q6.   Vegetative reproduction   [2 Marks]

Answer: It is the process by which new plants grow from parts of the parent plant other than seeds. It can happen through stems, roots, or leaves.

→ Definition of vegetative reproduction — 1 mark.

→ Any two correct plant parts (e.g., leaves, stems, roots) — 1 mark (0.5 each).

Marking: Definition (1 mark) + Two parts (0.5+0.5 mark). Accept: Stems and roots. Common Error: Mentioning flowers or seeds (these are sexual reproduction parts).

Ref: NCERT Class 5 Science

Q7. ★   Link structure to dispersal   [2 Marks]

Answer: Dandelion seeds have parachute-like hair so they can be easily carried away by the wind. Cocklebur seeds have hooks so they can attach to the fur of animals passing by and be carried to distant places.

→ Dandelion -> wind dispersal explanation — 1 mark.

→ Cocklebur -> animal dispersal explanation — 1 mark.

Marking: Explanation for both (1+1). Accept: Wind vs Animals. Common Error: Identifying the dispersal agent without linking it to the seed's physical structure.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 Science

Q8.   Internal structure of bean seed   [3 Marks]

Answer: Diagram showing the inner parts (Seed coat, Cotyledon, Embryo/Baby plant). The cotyledons store food for the baby plant until it can make its own food.

→ Neat, correct diagram forming a split bean — 1 mark.

→ Function of cotyledon: Stores food for the embryo — 1 mark.

Marking: Diagram (1 mark) + Labels (1 mark) + Function (1 mark). Accept: Baby plant for embryo. Common Error: Drawing a sprouting seed instead of the internal split parts.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 Science

Q9. ★   Germination conditions in drought   [3 Marks]

Answer: The three essential conditions for a seed to germinate are Water, Air (oxygen), and adequate Warmth (sunlight). In the case of dry soil during a drought, the missing condition was Water (moisture) which prevents the seed coat from softening and the embryo from growing.

→ Listing the 3 correct conditions (Water, Air, Warmth) — 2 marks (0.5 for two, full 2 for three).

→ Identifying Water/Moisture as the missing factor causing failure — 1 mark.

Marking: Conditions listed correctly (2 marks) + Application to drought setup (1 mark). Accept: Moisture for water. Common Error: Missing "Air" as a requirement.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 Science

SECTION D — 5 Marks Each

Q10.   Agriculture stages and Dispersal   [5 Marks]

Answer (a) — Agriculture [3 marks]:

→ Definition: Agriculture is the practice of growing plants and rearing animals on a large scale for food, clothing, and other needs. (1 mark)

→ Stages (any three): Ploughing/preparing the soil, Sowing healthy seeds, Adding manure/fertilizers, Irrigation (watering), Harvesting the crop. (2 marks for 3 stages)

Answer (b) — Dispersal [2 marks]:

→ Seed dispersal prevents overcrowding of plants in one place. (1 mark)

→ It ensures each plant gets enough sunlight, water, and soil nutrients, increasing the chances of survival for the new plants. (1 mark)

Marking: Part (a): Agriculture definition (1 mark) + 3 stages listed (2 marks). Part (b): Overcrowding prevention (1 mark) + Resource availability (1 mark). Accept: Plausible synonyms for agricultural stages. Common Error: Providing agricultural stages without explaining seed dispersal reasoning.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 Science
