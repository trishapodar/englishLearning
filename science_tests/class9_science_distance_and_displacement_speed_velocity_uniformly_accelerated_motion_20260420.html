SECTION A — 1 Mark(s) Each

Q1.   Zero   [1 Mark(s)]

Zero. The athlete returns to the starting point, so the net change in position is zero.

Marking: 1 mark for zero/0 m with or without unit.
Accept: 0, 0 m
Common Error: Students confuse displacement with distance (2πr ≈ 440 m) and give a non-zero answer.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8 (Motion), Section 8.1

Q2.   40 km/h   [1 Mark(s)]

Average speed = Total distance ÷ Total time = 60 ÷ 1.5 = 40 km/h.

Marking: 1 mark for 40 km/h (or equivalent in m/s ≈ 11.1 m/s).
Accept: 40 km h⁻¹, 11.1 m/s
Common Error: Dividing time by distance instead of distance by time.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Section 8.2

Q3.   Speed   [1 Mark(s)]

Speed (or average speed over the interval).

Marking: 1 mark for speed.
Accept: Speed, average speed
Common Error: Saying acceleration; acceleration is the slope of a velocity–time graph, not a distance–time graph.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Section 8.3

Q4.   v = gt   [1 Mark(s)]

Using the first equation of motion with initial velocity \(u = 0\):

\(v = u + at \Rightarrow v = 0 + gt \Rightarrow \boxed{v = gt}\)

Marking: 1 mark for v = gt (or v = 0 + gt).
Accept: v = u + gt with u = 0 clearly stated
Common Error: Writing v = g/t (dividing instead of multiplying).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Section 8.5

Q5.   Vector – has direction   [1 Mark(s)]

Displacement is a vector quantity because it has both magnitude and a specific direction (the shortest path from initial to final position).

Marking: ½ mark for vector; ½ mark for mentioning direction.
Accept: Any correct reason referencing direction.
Common Error: Calling displacement a scalar or confusing it with distance.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Section 8.1

SECTION B — 2 Mark(s) Each

Q6.   48 km/h; 0   [2 Mark(s)]

(i) Average speed:

Total distance = 120 + 120 = 240 km; Total time = 2 + 3 = 5 h

Average speed = 240 ÷ 5 = 48 km/h

(ii) Average velocity:

Total displacement = 0 (returns to starting point)

Average velocity = 0 ÷ 5 = 0 km/h

Marking: 1 mark for correct average speed (48 km/h). 1 mark for average velocity = 0 with reasoning (displacement = 0).
Accept: 13.3 m/s for speed; 0 m/s for velocity
Common Error: Adding the two speeds and dividing by 2, giving 50 km/h (arithmetic-mean trap).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Section 8.2

Q7.   20 m/s; displacement   [2 Mark(s)]

First equation of motion: \(v = u + at\)

\(v = 0 + 2 \times 10 = \mathbf{20 \text{ m/s}}\)

Area under velocity–time graph represents the displacement of the object.

Marking: 1 mark for v = 20 m/s. 1 mark for 'displacement' (penalise if student writes 'distance').
Accept: 20 m s⁻¹
Common Error: Writing 'distance' instead of displacement for the area under the v–t graph.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Sections 8.4–8.5

SECTION C — 3 Mark(s) Each

Q8.   Graph + 3.85 m/s   [3 Mark(s)]

(a) Distance–Time Graph:

→ Segment 1 (0–60 s): straight line from (0, 0) to (60, 300) — constant speed.

→ Segment 2 (60–90 s): horizontal line at distance = 300 m — stationary.

→ Segment 3 (90–130 s): straight line from (90, 300) to (130, 500) — constant speed.

(b) Average speed:

Total distance = 300 + 200 = 500 m; Total time = 60 + 30 + 40 = 130 s

Average speed = 500 ÷ 130 ≈ 3.85 m/s

Marking: 1 mark for correct graph with all three segments and labelled axes. 1 mark for correct total distance and time. 1 mark for final answer 3.85 m/s.
Accept: 3.8 m/s, 500/130 m/s
Common Error: Omitting the horizontal (stationary) segment or adding the 30 s stop only to one segment.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Section 8.3

Q9.   2 s; 20 m; 20 m/s downward   [3 Mark(s)]

At maximum height, \(v = 0\); \(u = 20\) m/s; \(a = -10\) m/s².

(a) Time to reach maximum height:

\(v = u + at \Rightarrow 0 = 20 - 10t \Rightarrow t = \mathbf{2 \text{ s}}\)

(b) Maximum height:

\(v^2 = u^2 + 2as \Rightarrow 0 = 400 - 20s \Rightarrow s = \mathbf{20 \text{ m}}\)

(c) Velocity on return to projection point:

By energy conservation / symmetry: \(v = \mathbf{20 \text{ m/s, downward}}\)

Marking: 1 mark for (a) t = 2 s. 1 mark for (b) s = 20 m. 1 mark for (c) 20 m/s downward — direction must be stated.
Accept: −20 m/s if upward is positive and convention is stated
Common Error: Omitting the direction in part (c), giving only the magnitude 20 m/s.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Section 8.5; NCERT Exemplar Ch. 8

SECTION D — 5 Mark(s) Each

Q10.   Graph; 300 m; 4 m/s²; No (50 m < 150 m)   [5 Mark(s)]

(a) Velocity–Time Graph:

→ Phase 1 (0 to 5 s): straight line from (0, 0) to (5, 20) — uniform acceleration.

→ Phase 2 (5 to 15 s): horizontal line at v = 20 m/s — constant velocity.

→ Phase 3 (15 to 20 s): straight line from (15, 20) to (20, 0) — uniform deceleration.

(b) Total distance = area under v–t graph:

Phase 1 (triangle): ½ × 5 × 20 = 50 m

Phase 2 (rectangle): 10 × 20 = 200 m

Phase 3 (triangle): ½ × 5 × 20 = 50 m

Total = 50 + 200 + 50 = 300 m

(c) Deceleration in Phase 3:

\(a = \frac{v - u}{t} = \frac{0 - 20}{5} = -4 \text{ m/s}^2\) → deceleration = 4 m/s²

(d) Distance covered in Phase 1:

\(s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 = 0 + \frac{1}{2}(4)(25) = 50 \text{ m}\)

Since 50 m < 150 m, the car has not passed the pedestrian by the end of Phase 1.

Marking: 1 mark for correctly labelled v–t graph (all 3 phases, correct vertices). 2 marks for total distance = 300 m (award 1 if method correct but arithmetic error). 1 mark for deceleration = 4 m/s². 1 mark for correct calculation of 50 m and justified conclusion 'No'.
Accept: Area method or kinematic equations both acceptable for part (b).
Common Error: Using the entire 20 s in a single equation; using rectangle instead of triangle for Phases 1 and 3.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Science, Ch. 8, Sections 8.4–8.5
