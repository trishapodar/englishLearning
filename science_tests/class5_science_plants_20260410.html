Q6. ★   [2 Marks]

Answer:

Reason 1: Without sunlight, the plant could not perform photosynthesis, so it stopped making food (glucose). The leaves had no energy to produce or maintain chlorophyll. (1 mark)

Reason 2: Without sunlight, the existing chlorophyll in the leaves started to break down and was not replaced. This caused the leaves to lose their green colour and turn pale yellow, a process called etiolation. (1 mark)

Marking: 1 mark per correct, well-explained reason. Accept scientific reasoning even if specific vocabulary (etiolation) is not used.

Accept: Any two of: no photosynthesis, chlorophyll breakdown, no food production, no sunlight for energy.

Common Error: Saying the plant "died" — the question says leaves turned yellow, implying the plant was still alive but stressed. Students should explain the process, not just state an endpoint.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 EVS "Looking Around", Ch. 7 — Experiment with Water; NCERT Exemplar, Plants and Photosynthesis.

Q8. ★   [3 Marks]

Answer:

(i) Yes — all food ultimately comes from plants. Example: When we eat a roti (wheat), the wheat grain came from the wheat plant. Even when we eat milk, eggs, or meat, the animals producing these had eaten plants or other plant-eating animals. (1 mark — agreement with valid example)

(ii) A plant makes food in its leaves using: sunlight (energy), water (absorbed by roots from soil), and carbon dioxide (absorbed from air through tiny pores called stomata). The plant converts these into glucose (sugar) and releases oxygen. (1 mark — correct raw materials)

(iii) Photosynthesis. (1 mark)

Marking: 1 mark per sub-part. Award part (i) mark if the example logically connects food to a plant source.

Accept: Any valid daily food item traced back to a plant source for (i). For (ii), essential requirements are: sunlight + water + CO₂.

Common Error: (ii) Many students say "soil" as a raw material — soil is the medium, the actual raw materials absorbed from it are water and dissolved minerals. Also, students often forget to mention sunlight.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 EVS "Looking Around", Ch. 7 — Experiment with Water; Ch. 4 — Mangoes Round the Year.

SECTION D — 5 Marks

Q9. ★   [5 Marks]

Answer — 1 mark per sub-part:

(i) Venus Flytrap: The soil in which the Venus flytrap grows is deficient in nitrogen. Nitrogen is essential for plants to make proteins and grow. Since it cannot get enough nitrogen from the soil, it traps insects, which are digested to supply the plant with the nitrogen it needs. (1 mark)

(ii) Cactus adaptations: The thick, fleshy stem stores water to survive long dry periods. The spine-like (modified) leaves reduce the surface area from which water can evaporate, helping the plant conserve water in the desert heat. (1 mark)

(iii) Water hyacinth: It is an aquatic plant. It absorbs dissolved mineral nutrients directly from the water through its submerged roots floating in the water. Sunlight falling on its leaves allows it to photosynthesise and make food. (1 mark)

(iv) Banyan tree's hanging roots: These are called prop roots (or aerial roots). They grow downward from the branches, enter the soil, and provide extra support to the heavy, wide-spreading branches of the banyan tree. Over time, they also absorb water and nutrients from the soil. (1 mark)

(v) Cuscuta (Amarbel): Cuscuta is a parasitic plant. It has no chlorophyll and cannot make its own food. It coils around a host plant and inserts thread-like suckers (haustoria) into the host's stem to absorb water, food, and nutrients directly from the host plant. (1 mark)

Marking: 1 mark per sub-part. Each answer must demonstrate understanding of the concept, not just recall of the name.

Accept: (ii) Any two correct adaptations of the cactus. (iii) Reference to water as the nutrient source. (iv) "Aerial roots" accepted alongside "prop roots" for banyan.

Common Error:
(i) Saying the plant is carnivorous "because it likes meat" — must link to nitrogen deficiency.
(v) Calling Cuscuta a creeper or climber — it is specifically a parasite; it does not photosynthesise.
(iv) Calling them "hanging roots" only — the specific function (support + nutrient absorption) must be stated.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 EVS "Looking Around", Ch. 4 — Mangoes Round the Year; NCERT Exemplar — Special Plants (parasitic, insectivorous, aquatic adaptations).
