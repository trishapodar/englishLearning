SECTION B — 2 Mark(s) Each

Q6.   Aeration & root penetration   [2 Mark(s)]

Loosening the soil is important because:

1. It allows the roots to breathe easily as air gets trapped in the loose soil.

2. It helps the roots penetrate deeper into the ground.

Marking: 1 mark for breathing/aeration. 1 mark for root penetration.
Accept: Helps seeds germinate better.
Common Error: Mentioning watering instead of root mobility.

Ref: NCERT Class 5, Seeds and Seeds / Agriculture

Q7.   Same flower vs Different flower   [2 Mark(s)]

→ Self-pollination: Pollen transfers from the anther to the stigma of the same flower (or another flower of the same plant).

→ Cross-pollination: Pollen transfers from the anther to the stigma of a flower on a different plant of the same kind.

Marking: 1 mark for correctly defining self-pollination. 1 mark for cross-pollination.
Accept: Simple descriptions using insects transferring pollen.
Common Error: Confusing pollination with fertilization.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 Science

SECTION C — 3 Mark(s) Each

Q8.   Germination & its 3 conditions   [3 Mark(s)]

Riya is trying to sprout the seeds (or achieve seed germination).

For germination to happen, seeds strictly require three conditions:

1. The right amount of water/moisture.

2. The right amount of air (oxygen).

3. The right temperature (warmth).

Marking: 1 mark for identifying germination/sprouting. 2 marks for stating the 3 conditions (water, air, warmth).
Accept: Moisture instead of water.
Common Error: Adding 'soil' or 'sunlight' as mandatory conditions for initial germination.

Ref: NCERT Class 5, Seeds and Seeds

Q9.   Equation & Oxygen release   [3 Mark(s)]

Word Equation: Carbon dioxide + Water → (in presence of Sunlight & Chlorophyll) → Glucose (Food) + Oxygen.

→ Plants act as air purifiers because they take in harmful Carbon dioxide gas from the environment and release fresh Oxygen gas, which living beings need to breathe.

Marking: 2 marks for the correct equation with sunlight/chlorophyll. 1 mark for explaining CO2 intake and O2 release.
Accept: Food/Sugars instead of Glucose.
Common Error: Saying plants consume oxygen instead of carbon dioxide.

Ref: NCERT Class 5 Science

SECTION D — 5 Mark(s) Each

Q10.   Dispersal, Farming Steps, Vegetative   [5 Mark(s)]

(a) Agents of seed dispersal: Wind, water, animals (including birds/humans), and exploding pods.

(b) Farming steps: 1. Ploughing/preparing soil, 2. Sowing seeds, 3. Adding manure/fertilizers, 4. Irrigation (watering), 5. Weeding, 6. Harvesting the ripe crop.

(c) Crops grown without seeds: Potato (from stem tubers), Rose (from stem cuttings), or Sweet potato (from roots).

Marking: 1 mark for dispersal agents. 3 marks for writing at least 4-5 correct farming steps in order. 1 mark for giving two non-seed crops.
Accept: Sugarcane, onions for part (c).
Common Error: Providing random fruit trees for part (c) that actually use seeds.

Ref: NCERT Class 5, Seeds and Seeds & Agriculture
