Q8.   Hunted for body parts; National Parks   [3 Mark(s)]

→ Elephants are hunted for their tusks, and rhinoceros are hunted for their horns.

→ The hunters who kill them for these valuable body parts are called poachers.

→ To protect them, the government has established protected areas like National Parks (e.g., Jim Corbett National Park) where hunting or catching animals is strictly prohibited.

Marking: 1 mark for identifying why elephants/rhinos are hunted. 1 mark for explaining the threat of poachers. 1 mark for mentioning government action (National Parks).
Accept: Mentioning wildlife sanctuaries or specific laws.
Common Error: Saying they are hunted for meat, or forgetting to mention National Parks.

Ref: NCERT Class 5, Super Senses

Q9.   Loss of spatial awareness   [3 Mark(s)]

If a tiger's whiskers are trimmed, it will severely struggle to hunt and navigate in the dark.

→ Tiger's whiskers are extremely sensitive and can sense the slightest movements or vibrations in the air.

→ They help the tiger judge distances, feel its way in absolute darkness, and exactly locate its prey.

Marking: 1 mark for stating the struggle to hunt. 2 marks for explaining the functional role of whiskers (sensing vibrations, finding prey in the dark).
Accept: Valid explanations on tactile senses.
Common Error: Concluding the tiger will go blind, or confusing whiskers with hearing.

Ref: NCERT Class 5, Super Senses

Q10.   Smell trails disruption   [5 Mark(s)]

(a) The ants will most likely climb up and over the pencil block to continue their journey.

(b) As ants move, they leave a distinct smell on the ground. The trailing ants follow this specific smell trail to find their way.

(c) Silkworm (can find its female from many kilometres away by her smell).

(d) If Ravi wipes the floor with a strong cleaner, the chemical smell of the cleaner will mask or destroy the ant's scent trail. The trailing ants will lose their way and scatter.

Marking: 1 mark for (a). 2 marks for (b) mentioning scent trails. 1 mark for (c) Silkworm. 1 mark for (d) concluding the ants will scatter/lose their way.
Accept: Logical conclusions about destroying the pheromone trail.
Common Error: Assuming ants rely on footprints or memory instead of smell.
