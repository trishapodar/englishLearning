SECTION A — 1 Mark Each

Q1.   [1 Mark]

Answer: 6 sides.

Marking: Correct answer — 1 mark. No partial marks.

Common Error: Confusing hexagon (6 sides) with pentagon (5 sides).

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths (Math-Magic), Ch. 2 & general shape vocabulary.

Q2.   [1 Mark]

Answer: Square.

Marking: Correct answer — 1 mark. No partial marks.

Common Error: Writing "rectangle" — a rectangle has 4 right angles but NOT necessarily 4 equal sides.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Math-Magic, Ch. 13 — Fields and Fences (shape properties).

Q3.   [1 Mark]

Answer: True.

Marking: "True" written clearly — 1 mark.

Common Error: Writing "False" by confusing 'corner' with 'angle'; a circle has neither sides nor corners.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Math-Magic, shape properties (introductory geometry).

Q4.   [1 Mark]

Answer: 5 vertices.

Marking: Correct answer — 1 mark.

Common Error: Confusing number of sides (5) with number of vertices — they are the same for a polygon, but students must recall the term "vertex".

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Math-Magic, Ch. 13 — Fields and Fences.

Q5.   [2 Marks]

SquareRectangle

Q6. ★   [2 Marks]

Answer:

Perimeter of square = 4 × side = 4 × 20 cm = 80 cm.

Priya needs 80 cm of border ribbon.

Marking:

  Step 1 — Correct formula: Perimeter = 4 × side — (1 mark)

  Final answer: 80 cm with unit — (1 mark)

Accept: Adding 20 + 20 + 20 + 20 = 80 cm (repeated addition method).

Common Error: Forgetting to include all 4 sides; computing only 2 × 20 = 40 cm.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Math-Magic, Ch. 13 — Fields and Fences, Perimeter of a Square.

SECTION C — 3 Marks Each

Q7.   [3 Marks]

Answer:

(i) Shapes with exactly 4 sides: Rectangle and Square. (1 mark — both required)

(ii) Shape with the most sides: Hexagon (6 sides). (1 mark)

(iii) Shape with 3 vertices: Triangle. Drawing: a neat closed 3-sided figure with vertices labelled A, B, C. (1 mark — name + acceptable drawing)

Marking: 1 mark per sub-part. For (iii), award mark if triangle is clearly identified and drawn even without labelling.

Accept: Any triangular shape (equilateral, isosceles, scalene) for part (iii).

Common Error: (i) Naming only one shape; (ii) selecting pentagon instead of hexagon.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Math-Magic, Ch. 13 — Fields and Fences; shape properties across Ch. 2.

Q8. ★   [3 Marks]

Answer:

(i) Neat labelled sketch: rectangle with Length = 9 m, Breadth = 5 m. (1 mark)

(ii) Perimeter of rectangular flower bed = 2 × (length + breadth) = 2 × (9 + 5) = 2 × 14 = 28 m. (1 mark)

(iii) Fencing along 3 sides (both breadths + one length) = 5 + 5 + 9 = 19 m. (1 mark)

Marking: 1 mark per sub-part. Award method mark for (ii) and (iii) even if arithmetic is slightly off.

Accept: Perimeter computed as 9 + 5 + 9 + 5 = 28 m (adding all four sides).

Common Error: (iii) Computing perimeter of all 4 sides instead of only 3; or adding one breadth instead of two.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Math-Magic, Ch. 13 — Fields and Fences, Ex. 13.1.

SECTION D — 5 Marks

Q9. ★   [5 Marks]

Answer:

(i) Sketch: [1 mark]
A large rectangle labelled Length = 15 m, Breadth = 10 m, with a small square inside labelled 4 m × 4 m. Neat and labelled drawing earns 1 mark.

(ii) Perimeter of rectangular field: [1 mark]
= 2 × (Length + Breadth)
= 2 × (15 + 10)
= 2 × 25
= 50 m

(iii) Perimeter of square shed: [1 mark]
= 4 × side
= 4 × 4
= 16 m

(iv) Total cost of fencing the field: [1 mark]
Perimeter = 50 m (from part ii)
Cost = 50 × ₹10 = ₹500

(v) Total distance walked: [1 mark]
= Perimeter of field + Perimeter of shed
= 50 + 16
= 66 m

Marking: 1 mark per sub-part. Award method mark for correct formula even if calculation error occurs.

Accept:
(ii) 15 + 10 + 15 + 10 = 50 m (adding all sides).
(iii) 4 + 4 + 4 + 4 = 16 m.
(iv) Any correct multiplication method.

Common Error:
(iv) Using breadth instead of perimeter; multiplying ₹10 by only length or only one side.
(v) Adding only the two perimeters without recognising they are separate shapes.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Math-Magic, Ch. 13 — Fields and Fences, Ex. 13.1 and 13.2.
