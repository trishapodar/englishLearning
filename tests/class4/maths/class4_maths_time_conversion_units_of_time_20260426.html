SECTION A — 1 Mark(s) Each

Q1.   Seconds; 60 seconds per round   [1 Mark(s)]

The third hand measures seconds.

→ It completes one full round of the clock face in 60 seconds.

Marking: ½ mark for 'seconds'; ½ mark for '60 seconds per round'. Award 1 mark for a fully correct answer.
Accept: '1 minute' accepted in place of '60 seconds' for the duration.
Common Error: Students confuse the minute hand with the seconds hand, or state the seconds hand takes 60 minutes.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3 (How Many Squares?) — Time chapter; NCERT Exemplar Class 4.

Q2.   19:30   [1 Mark(s)]

7:30 PM → Add 12 to the hours: 7 + 12 = 19

→ 19:30

Marking: 1 mark for writing 19:30 correctly. No mark for 7:30 without conversion.
Accept: 19:30 or 1930 hrs.
Common Error: Students write 17:30 (subtracting instead of adding 12), or forget to add 12 for PM times after noon.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; NCERT Exemplar Class 4.

Q3.   105 minutes   [1 Mark(s)]

1 hour = 60 minutes

→ 1 hour 45 minutes = 60 + 45 = 105 minutes

Marking: 1 mark for 105 minutes. Accept working shown.
Accept: 105 min.
Common Error: Students write 145 minutes (concatenating digits instead of converting hours to minutes).

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; conversion of time units.

Q4.   2 hours   [1 Mark(s)]

12:00 − 10:00 = 2 hours

Marking: 1 mark for 2 hours. Accept '120 minutes'.
Accept: 2 hours or 120 minutes.
Common Error: Students confuse 12:00 (noon) with midnight and subtract incorrectly.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; subtraction of time, use of 24-hour clock.

SECTION B — 2 Mark(s) Each

Q5.   2 hours 35 minutes   [2 Mark(s)]

Divide 155 by 60:

→ 155 ÷ 60 = 2 remainder 35

→ 2 hours 35 minutes

Marking: 1 mark for correct division (155 ÷ 60 = 2 r 35); 1 mark for correct final answer (2 hr 35 min).
Accept: 2 h 35 min; 2 hours and 35 minutes.
Common Error: Students divide 155 by 100 instead of 60, giving wrong hours.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; conversion of time using different units.

Q6.   7 hours 15 minutes   [2 Mark(s)]

Minutes: 40 + 35 = 75 min = 1 hour 15 minutes (carry 1 hour)

Hours: 4 + 2 + 1 (carried) = 7 hours

→ 7 hours 15 minutes

Marking: 1 mark for correctly handling the carry-over from minutes; 1 mark for the correct final answer.
Accept: 7 hr 15 min; 7 h 15 m.
Common Error: Students write 6 hours 75 minutes without converting 75 minutes to 1 hour 15 minutes.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; addition of time.

Q7.   35 minutes   [2 Mark(s)]

08:20 − 07:45

→ Minutes: 20 − 45 is not possible; borrow 1 hour → 80 − 45 = 35 minutes

→ Hours: 8 − 1 (borrowed) − 7 = 0 hours

→ 35 minutes

Marking: 1 mark for correct subtraction method; 1 mark for final answer 35 minutes.
Accept: 35 min.
Common Error: Students subtract without borrowing and write 75 minutes or −25 minutes.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; subtraction of time.

Q8.   (i) 12:05 AM (ii) 3:40 PM   [2 Mark(s)]

(i) 00:05 → 12:05 AM (midnight hour)

(ii) 15:40 → 15 − 12 = 3 → 3:40 PM

Marking: 1 mark for each correct conversion (including AM/PM label).
Accept: 12:05 AM and 03:40 PM.
Common Error: Students write 00:05 as 12:05 PM (wrong period) or write 15:40 as 5:40 PM (subtracting wrong value).

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; 24-hour clock, use of AM and PM.

SECTION C — 3 Mark(s) Each

Q9.   1590 minutes   [3 Mark(s)]

Step 1: 1 day = 24 hours → 24 × 60 = 1440 minutes

Step 2: 2 hours = 2 × 60 = 120 minutes

Step 3: 30 minutes = 30 minutes

→ Total = 1440 + 120 + 30 = 1590 minutes

Marking: 1 mark for converting 1 day correctly; 1 mark for converting 2 hours correctly; 1 mark for final sum 1590 minutes.
Accept: 1590 min.
Common Error: Students convert 1 day to 12 hours instead of 24 hours.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; conversion of time using different units (days, hours, minutes).

Q10.   (i) 2:25 PM (ii) 14:25   [3 Mark(s)]

Start: 10:45 AM + 3 hours 40 minutes

→ Minutes: 45 + 40 = 85 min = 1 hr 25 min (carry 1 hr)

→ Hours: 10 + 3 + 1 = 14 (24-hour), which is 2 PM

(i) End time: 2:25 PM

(ii) 24-hour format: 14:25

Marking: 1 mark for correct minute addition with carry; 1 mark for end time in AM/PM (2:25 PM); 1 mark for 24-hour format (14:25).
Accept: 14:25 hrs; 2:25 PM.
Common Error: Students forget to carry over the extra hour from minutes, writing 2:05 PM or 13:25.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; addition of time, use of AM and PM, 24-hour clock.

Q11.   2 hours 25 minutes   [3 Mark(s)]

Minutes: 10 − 45 is not possible; borrow 1 hour from hours.

→ 10 + 60 = 70 minutes; 70 − 45 = 25 minutes

→ Hours: 6 − 1 (borrowed) = 5; 5 − 3 = 2 hours

→ Answer: 2 hours 25 minutes

Marking: 1 mark for identifying need to borrow; 1 mark for correct minute subtraction (25 min); 1 mark for correct hour calculation (2 hr).
Accept: 2 hr 25 min.
Common Error: Students subtract minutes directly (45 − 10 = 35) reversing the order, giving 2 hours 35 minutes.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; subtraction of time.

Q12.   (i) 8 circles (ii) 360°   [3 Mark(s)]

(i) Time from 9:00 AM to 9:08 AM = 8 minutes = 8 × 60 = 480 seconds

→ Circles = 480 ÷ 60 = 8 full circles

(ii) One full circle = 360 degrees

Marking: 1 mark for converting 8 minutes to 480 seconds; 1 mark for dividing by 60 to get 8 circles; 1 mark for stating 360°.
Accept: 8 rounds / revolutions; 360°.
Common Error: Students divide 8 (minutes) by 60 instead of multiplying, getting a fraction.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; third hand (second hand) of clock.

Q13.   (i) 40 minutes (ii) 3:20 PM   [3 Mark(s)]

(i) 08:30 − 07:50

→ Minutes: 30 − 50 not possible; borrow 1 hr: 90 − 50 = 40 minutes

→ Hours: 8 − 1 − 7 = 0 hours

→ Journey = 40 minutes

(ii) 15:20 → 15 − 12 = 3 → 3:20 PM

Marking: 1 mark for correct subtraction method; 1 mark for journey time (40 min); 1 mark for correct AM/PM conversion (3:20 PM).
Accept: 40 min; 03:20 PM.
Common Error: Students write 08:30 − 07:50 = 00:80 without borrowing, then fail to simplify.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; subtraction of time, 24-hour clock, use of AM and PM.

Q14.   5 hours 10 minutes   [3 Mark(s)]

Step 1 — Add all minutes: 50 + 35 + 45 = 130 minutes

→ 130 ÷ 60 = 2 hours remainder 10 minutes (carry 2 hours)

Step 2 — Add all hours: 2 + 1 + 0 + 2 (carried) = 5 hours

→ 5 hours 10 minutes

Marking: 1 mark for adding all minutes correctly (130); 1 mark for correct carry conversion (2 hr 10 min); 1 mark for final answer (5 hr 10 min).
Accept: 5 hr 10 min.
Common Error: Students add hours and minutes separately but forget to carry the extra hours from the minutes sum.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; addition of time.

SECTION D — 5 Mark(s) Each

Q15.   6 hr 30 min; 1 hr; 5 hr 30 min; 60 rounds   [5 Mark(s)]

(i) Total duration of school day:

→ 14:30 − 08:00 = 6 hours 30 minutes

(ii) Total break time:

→ 15 + 35 + 10 = 60 minutes = 1 hour

(iii) Actual teaching time:

→ 6 hr 30 min − 1 hr 0 min = 5 hours 30 minutes

(iv) Rounds of second hand during breaks:

→ Total break = 60 minutes = 60 × 60 = 3600 seconds

→ Rounds = 3600 ÷ 60 = 60 full rounds

Marking: 1 mark for (i) correct school day duration; 1 mark for (ii) total break time; 1 mark for (iii) teaching time; 1 mark for converting break time to seconds; 1 mark for (iv) correct rounds (60).
Accept: 6 h 30 min; 60 min for break; 5 h 30 min; 60 rounds/revolutions.
Common Error: Students compute break time in hours incorrectly (e.g., 1.5 hr) or forget to convert minutes to seconds before dividing for part (iv).

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; all time sub-topics — 24-hour clock, subtraction, addition, third hand, conversion.

Q16.   Train P: 7 hr 40 min; Train Q: 7 hr 50 min; Train Q longer by 10 min   [5 Mark(s)]

(i) Train P's journey (crosses midnight):

→ Time from 22:30 to midnight (24:00) = 24:00 − 22:30 = 1 hour 30 minutes

→ Time from midnight to 06:10 = 6 hours 10 minutes

→ Total = 1 hr 30 min + 6 hr 10 min = 7 hours 40 minutes

(ii) Train Q:

→ Departure: 09:15 AM → 09:15 (24-hr)

→ Arrival: 5:05 PM → 5 + 12 = 17 → 17:05 (24-hr)

→ 17:05 − 09:15

→ Minutes: 05 − 15 not possible; borrow 1 hr: 65 − 15 = 50 minutes

→ Hours: 17 − 1 − 9 = 7 hours

→ Train Q's journey = 7 hours 50 minutes

(iii) Comparison:

→ 7 hr 50 min − 7 hr 40 min = 10 minutes

→ Train Q has a longer journey by 10 minutes.

Marking: 1 mark for correctly handling midnight crossing for Train P; 1 mark for Train P total (7 hr 40 min); 1 mark for correct 24-hr conversions of Train Q; 1 mark for Train Q duration (7 hr 50 min); 1 mark for correct comparison and difference (Train Q, 10 min).
Accept: 7 h 40 min; 7 h 50 min; Q is longer by 10 min.
Common Error: Students calculate Train P's journey as 22:30 to 06:10 by simple subtraction (giving negative or wrong answer) without accounting for the midnight crossing.

Ref: NCERT Class 4 Maths, Ch. 3; subtraction of time across midnight, 24-hour clock, conversion, AM/PM.
