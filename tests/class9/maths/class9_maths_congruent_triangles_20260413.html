Q4.   Isosceles angles property   [1 Mark]

Answer: ∠A = 70°.

Working: Since AB = AC, ∠C = ∠B = 55°. Sum of angles = 55 + 55 + ∠A = 180° ⇒ ∠A = 180 - 110 = 70°.

Marking: 1 mark for the final correct answer 70°. Accept: 70°. Common Error: Saying the vertex angle is 55° or miscalculating the supplement.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 7

Marking: 1 mark for full correct expansion. Accept: Right-Hypotenuse-Side. Common Error: Right Hand Side.

SECTION B — 2 Marks Each

Q6.   Altitude bisecting the base   [2 Marks]

Answer: In ΔABD and ΔACD:
Condition 1: BD = CD (Given, since AD bisects BC).
Condition 2: ∠ADB = ∠ADC = 90° (Given, since AD is altitude/perpendicular).
Condition 3: AD = AD (Common side).
Therefore, by SAS congruence criterion, ΔABD ≅ ΔACD.

Marking: 1 mark for listing the 3 correct parts. 1 mark for stating SAS and concluding congruence. Accept: RHS (since hypotenuse isn't known equal yet, RHS is actually incorrect here—must be SAS). Common Error: Attempting to use RHS before proving AB=AC.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 7

Q7. ★   Perpendicular bisector locus   [2 Marks]

Answer: Let M be the intersection of l and AB. Since line l is the perpendicular bisector, AM = BM and ∠PMA = ∠PMB = 90°.
In ΔPMA and ΔPMB:
AM = BM (Given)
∠PMA = ∠PMB = 90° (Given)
PM = PM (Common)
By SAS criterion, ΔPMA ≅ ΔPMB.
By CPCT (Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles), PA = PB. Hence, P is equidistant from A and B.

Marking: 1 mark for setting up the triangles/diagram. 1 mark for SAS and CPCT application. Accept: Right angle definition directly. Common Error: Missing CPCT statement at the end.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 7

SECTION C — 3 Marks Each

Q8.   Parallel lines and midpoint   [3 Marks]

Answer:
(i) In ΔAOB and ΔDOC:
∠OAB = ∠ODC (Alternate interior angles since AB || CD and AD is transversal) — 0.5
OA = OD (Given, O is mid-point of AD) — 0.5
∠AOB = ∠DOC (Vertically opposite angles) — 0.5
Therefore, by ASA (or AAS) congruence rule, ΔAOB ≅ ΔDOC. — 0.5

(ii) Because the triangles are congruent, their corresponding parts are equal. Thus, OB = OC (by CPCT), which means O is also the mid-point of BC. — 1

Marking: 2 marks for part (i) listing criteria, 1 mark for part (ii) CPCT deduction. Accept: AAS rule using the other set of alternate interior angles. Common Error: Forgetting to cite vertical angles or parallel line angle theorems.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 7, Example 1

Q9. ★   Isosceles Triangle Theorem proof   [3 Marks]

Answer: Given an isosceles triangle ABC where AB = AC. We need to prove ∠B = ∠C.
Construction: Draw the bisector of ∠A, let it meet BC at D.
In ΔABD and ΔACD:
AB = AC (Given) — 0.5
∠BAD = ∠CAD (By construction) — 1
AD = AD (Common side) — 0.5
By SAS congruence criterion, ΔABD ≅ ΔACD. — 0.5
Therefore, ∠ABD = ∠ACD (or ∠B = ∠C) by CPCT. — 0.5

Marking: 1 mark for correct construction. 1.5 marks for listing SAS parts. 0.5 for conclusion via CPCT. Accept: Construction of median or altitude instead of angle bisector (using SSS or RHS respectively). Common Error: Circular reasoning (using ∠B = ∠C to prove the triangles congruent in order to prove ∠B = ∠C).

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 7, Theorem 7.2

SECTION D — 5 Marks Each

Q10.   5-Part Congruence sequence in Right Triangle   [5 Marks]

Answer:

(i) In ΔAMC and ΔBMD:
AM = BM (M is the mid-point of AB)
∠AMC = ∠BMD (Vertically opposite angles)
CM = DM (Given)
By SAS criterion, ΔAMC ≅ ΔBMD. (1.5 Marks)

(ii) Since ΔAMC ≅ ΔBMD, we know ∠ACM = ∠BDM by CPCT.
These are alternate interior angles formed by transversal CD intersecting AC and BD, so AC || BD.
Because they are parallel, consecutive interior angles are supplementary: ∠ACB + ∠DBC = 180°.
Given ∠ACB = 90°, so 90° + ∠DBC = 180° ⇒ ∠DBC = 90°. (1.5 Marks)

(iii) In ΔDBC and ΔACB:
DB = AC (by CPCT from the first pair of triangles)
∠DBC = ∠ACB = 90° (Proved above)
BC = CB (Common base)
By SAS criterion, ΔDBC ≅ ΔACB. (1 Mark)

(iv) Since ΔDBC ≅ ΔACB, by CPCT, their hypotenuses are equal: DC = AB.
We know CM = ½ DC (because M is mid-point of CD, DM = CM).
Substituting DC with AB, we get CM = ½ AB. (1 Mark)

Marking: Stepwise distribution. i (1.5), ii (1.5), iii (1), iv (1). Accept: Alternate valid geometric steps for parallel lines. Common Error: Skipping step (ii) entirely and trying to jump straight to (iii) without proving the $90^\circ$ angle.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Maths, Ch. 7, Ex 7.1 Q8
