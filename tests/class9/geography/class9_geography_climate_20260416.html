1. Weather refers to the state of the atmosphere over an area at any point of time.

2. Climate refers to the sum total of weather conditions and variations over a large area for a long period of time.

SECTION C — 3 Mark(s) Each

Q7.   Climatic Controls   [3 Mark(s)]

1. Latitude: The Tropic of Cancer passes through the middle of the country, dividing it into tropical and subtropical climate zones.

2. Altitude: The Himalayas prevent the cold winds from Central Asia from entering the subcontinent, resulting in comparatively milder winters.

3. Pressure and Winds: Unique pressure conditions, varying from winter to summer, result in seasonal reversal of winds carrying moisture to Indian landmass.

Marking: 1 mark for each factor explained.
Accept: Other factors like distance from the sea or relief.
Common Error: Only listing factors without brief explanations.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Geography, Ch. 4

Q8.   Regional Variations   [3 Mark(s)]

Despite an overall monsoon type of climate, India exhibits large regional variations.

1. Temperature variation: In summer, mercury occasionally touches 50°C in parts of Rajasthan desert, whereas it may be around 20°C in Pahalgam in Jammu and Kashmir. In winter, night temperature at Drass may be -45°C. Thiruvananthapuram may have 22°C.

2. Precipitation variation: The annual precipitation varies from over 400 cm in Meghalaya to less than 10 cm in Ladakh and western Rajasthan.

Marking: 1 mark for stating variation. 1 mark each for two contrasting examples (temperature and rainfall).
Accept: Any valid contrasting examples from NCERT.
Common Error: Providing vague non-quantified statements without specific regions.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Geography, Ch. 4

SECTION D — 5 Mark(s) Each

Q9.   Cold Weather Season   [5 Mark(s)]

The main characteristics of the cold weather season are:

1. Duration: Begins from mid-November in northern India and stays till February. December and January are the coldest.

2. Temperature: Decreases from south to the north. Frost is common in the north and higher slopes of the Himalayas experience snowfall.

3. Pressure and Winds: Northeast trade winds prevail over the country blowing from land to sea, meaning most parts experience a dry season.

4. Winter Rainfall: A slight amount of rainfall occurs on the Tamil Nadu coast due to northeast winds. Also, western cyclonic disturbances cause winter rainfall over the northern plains which is highly beneficial for rabi crops (locally known as 'mahawat').

5. Weather features: Normally marked by clear sky, low temperatures, low humidity, and feeble, variable winds.

Marking: 1 mark for each fully explained characteristic up to 5.
Accept: Valid points from NCERT.
Common Error: Forgetting to mention the western cyclonic disturbances.

Ref: NCERT Class 9 Geography, Ch. 4
