1. What is an Adjective?

An adjective is a word that describes, qualifies, or modifies a noun or pronoun.
It answers questions like: What kind? Which one? How many?

Examples:

  • She has a beautiful dress.
  • This is a red apple.
Mistake: Using adverb instead of adjective — "She is beautifully" ❌
Topper Note: Adjectives always attach meaning directly to nouns.

2. Types of Adjectives

  • Descriptive: tall, happy
  • Quantitative: some, much
  • Demonstrative: this, that
  • Possessive: my, your
  • Interrogative: which, what
Examiner Trap: "Some" can be pronoun or adjective depending on usage.

3. Order of Adjectives

Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose

Example: A beautiful small old round red Italian wooden table

Wrong order is a common exam trap.

4. Degrees of Comparison

Positive, Comparative, Superlative
  • tall → taller → tallest
  • good → better → best
Irregular forms are favorite exam questions.

5. Limiting Adjectives

They restrict nouns: few, many, several

"Few" vs "A few" difference is critical.

6. Deep Descriptive Usage

Stacking adjectives effectively without redundancy.

Avoid overloading adjectives in formal writing.

7. Participial Adjectives

-ing vs -ed forms

  • boring lecture
  • bored students
Confusion between feeling vs cause.

8. Compound Adjectives

well-known author, high-speed train

Hyphen usage is tested in exams.

9. Adjective vs Adverb

She is quick (adj) vs She runs quickly (adv)

10. Position of Adjectives

Before noun or after linking verbs

11. Intensifiers

very, too, enough

12. Advanced Usage

Elliptical adjectives, omitted nouns

13. Common Errors

  • More better ❌
  • Most unique ❌

14. Exam Traps

Double comparison, wrong order, misused participles

Practice Test

1. He is ___ than me.
a) tall b) taller c) tallest d) more tall
2. Choose correct order:
a) red big ball b) big red ball c) ball big red d) red ball big
3. Error detection:
She is more smarter than him.
4. Correction:
This is a most unique idea.
