Constitutional & Legal Developments

★ LANDMARK SC JUDGMENT · 24 MARCH

SC Grants Permanent Commission to Women Officers in Armed Forces

In a landmark ruling in Lt. Col. Pooja Pal & Others v. Union of India (2026 INSC 281), the Supreme Court found that systemic bias within the Army, Navy and Air Force had led to the denial of Permanent Commission (PC) to roughly 73 Short Service Commission officers — the vast majority of them women. The bench of CJI Surya Kant held this violates Articles 14 (equality) and 21 (dignity) and directed grant of permanent commission to the aggrieved officers.

  • Bench: CJI Surya Kant, Ujjal Bhuyan, N. Kotiswar Singh
  • Earlier SC ruling (2020): Women entitled to PC in Army on par with men — Annie Nagaraja case
  • Article 14: Equality before law | Article 16: Equal opportunity in public employment
  • Court condemned: The use of non-transparent, arbitrary evaluation methods for PC selection
  • Defence services fall under Union List — Parliament legislates on Armed Forces composition
CLAT Angle: Equality jurisprudence in public employment, Articles 14 & 16, and gender discrimination in state institutions are extremely high-frequency CLAT topics. This case will likely appear in reading comprehension passages.
LEGISLATION · SEPARATION OF POWERS · CRITICAL

CAPF (General Administration) Bill, 2026 Introduced in Rajya Sabha

The Central Government introduced the Central Armed Police Forces (General Administration) Bill, 2026 in the Rajya Sabha, codifying the role of IPS officers in Group 'A' command positions within CAPFs like BSF, CRPF, CISF, ITBP and SSB. The Bill directly overrides a 2025 Supreme Court direction (Sanjay Prakash case) that had asked the Government to progressively reduce IPS deputation at the Inspector-General rank and below within two years.

  • CAPFs covered: BSF, CRPF, CISF, ITBP, SSB, Assam Rifles (partially)
  • SC judgment overridden: 2025 Sanjay Prakash ruling on progressive reduction of IPS deputation
  • Constitutional tension: Parliament using legislative power to negate judicial direction
  • Article 50 (DPSP): Separation of judiciary from executive — principle being debated
  • Internal Security is a Union List subject (List I, Entry 2A)
CLAT Angle: Separation of powers, judicial review, and Parliament's competence to override judicial pronouncements through legislation are classic CLAT constitutional law questions.
HUMAN RIGHTS · CRIMINAL LAW · HIGH IMPORTANCE

India's Custodial Death Crisis: Over 5 Deaths/Day, No Anti-Torture Law

Despite over five custodial deaths per day on record, India continues to have no comprehensive anti-torture law. The most recent legislative attempt — the Prevention of Torture Bill — has lapsed in Parliament. India has also failed to ratify the UN Convention Against Torture (UNCAT), which it signed in 1997. The issue returned to national attention following the Sathankulam custodial death case verdict in Madurai, where a court found all nine police officers guilty.

  • India signed UNCAT in 1997 — but never ratified it (ratification makes it binding)
  • No definition of "torture" exists in the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023
  • Article 21: Right to life and personal liberty — protects against custodial torture
  • D.K. Basu v. State of West Bengal (1997): SC issued binding guidelines on arrest procedures to prevent custodial violence
  • Sathankulam Case (2020): Jaeyaraj and Fenix died in police custody — became a national outrage
CLAT Angle: Article 21, D.K. Basu guidelines, UNCAT, and the right against torture are foundational criminal law topics for CLAT. The contrast between India's international obligations and domestic legal gaps is excellent for comprehension passages.
NIA · TERRORISM LAW · 24 MARCH

NIA Court Sentences Kashmiri Separatist Asiya Andrabi to Life Imprisonment

A special NIA court sentenced Asiya Andrabi — founder of Dukhtaran-e-Millat, a pro-Pakistan separatist organization — to life imprisonment for involvement in terrorist activities in Kashmir, including funding terrorism and waging war against India. She had been in NIA custody since 2018.

  • Charges: UAPA (Unlawful Activities Prevention Act) and IPC Section 121 (waging war against India)
  • NIA: National Investigation Agency, set up under NIA Act 2008, investigates terror-related offences
  • UAPA, 1967: India's primary anti-terror law — amended in 2004, 2008, 2012, 2019
  • 2019 UAPA Amendment: Allows designation of individuals (not just organisations) as terrorists
  • Article 19(2): Reasonable restrictions on speech in interest of sovereignty and integrity of India
CLAT Angle: UAPA, NIA jurisdiction, and the tension between national security and fundamental rights are recurring CLAT themes. The 2019 amendment expanding individual designation has been challenged in court.
SUPREME COURT · SC/ST ACT · RESERVATION

SC Rules: Scheduled Caste Status Depends on Religion — Caste Conversion Controversy

In a significant ruling (2026 INSC 283), the Supreme Court reaffirmed that SC/ST status under the Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950 applies only to Hindus, Sikhs, and Buddhists — not to Christians or Muslims. A person who converted from a Scheduled Caste Hindu background to Christianity cannot invoke the SC/ST Act unless re-conversion is established, as the Constitutional Order does not extend SC status to non-Hindu, non-Sikh, and non-Buddhist communities.

  • Constitutional basis: President's Order of 1950 (as amended in 1956 and 1990) — SC status limited by religion
  • Para 3 of the Order: Only Hindus, Sikhs (added 1956) and Buddhists (added 1990) qualify as SCs
  • Pending matter: E.V. Chinnaiah v. State of AP (sub judice) — on SC status for Dalit Christians
  • National Commission for Religious & Linguistic Minorities (Ranganath Misra Report, 2007): Recommended extending SC status to Dalit Muslims and Christians — not implemented
CLAT Angle: The SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, reservation jurisprudence, and the intersection of religion and caste in constitutional law are extremely common CLAT topics.

Governance, Policy & Landmark Events

★ MEGA INFRASTRUCTURE · 28 MARCH

Noida International Airport (Jewar) Inaugurated by PM Modi

Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Phase 1 of the Noida International Airport (IATA: DXN) at Jewar, Gautam Buddha Nagar, Uttar Pradesh on March 28, 2026 — making it Delhi-NCR's second international airport and UP's fifth. Developed at a cost of ₹11,282 crore under the Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model, the airport was built by Noida International Airport Limited (NIAL) with Flughafen Zürich AG (operator of Zurich Airport) as the concessionaire for 40 years.

  • Phase 1 capacity: 1.2 crore (12 million) passengers per annum (MPPA)
  • Ultimate capacity (all phases): 70 MPPA by 2040 — among Asia's largest
  • IATA Code: DXN | ICAO: VIND | Foundation stone laid: November 25, 2021
  • Constructor: Tata Projects Limited | Concessionaire: Flughafen Zürich AG (40 years)
  • Launch airlines: IndiGo, Akasa Air, Air India Express
  • Airport is designed to operate as a net-zero emissions facility
  • PPP model: Government + Private sector collaboration for infrastructure development
CLAT Angle: PPP model for infrastructure, Airports Authority of India (AAI) Act, DGCA licensing, and aviation sector governance are relevant for GK passages. Also note airport-led urbanisation (aerotropolis concept) as an emerging theme.
LEGISLATION · CORPORATE LAW · 24 MARCH

Corporate Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2026 Referred to JPC

Introduced in the Lok Sabha by the Union Finance Minister, the Corporate Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2026 seeks to amend both the Companies Act, 2013 and the Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) Act, 2008. The Bill has been referred to a 31-member Joint Parliamentary Committee (JPC) for detailed scrutiny, with a report due by the Monsoon Session.

  • Key reform: Decriminalisation of minor corporate offences — converting criminal penalties to civil monetary fines
  • CSR threshold raised: From ₹5 crore to ₹10 crore net profit for CSR applicability
  • JPC composition: 21 MPs from Lok Sabha (nominated by Speaker) + 10 from Rajya Sabha
  • NFRA (National Financial Reporting Authority) gets stronger oversight role
  • AGMs/EGMs now permissible via videoconferencing
  • India's Ease of Doing Business rank: India is #63 in World Bank's DB 2020 (latest ranking)
CLAT Angle: Role of Joint Parliamentary Committees, Companies Act, CSR provisions under Section 135, and corporate governance are relevant for CLAT legal reasoning and GK sections.
RBI · DIGITAL PAYMENTS · 30 MARCH

RBI Releases "Payments Vision 2028" — Shared Liability for Digital Fraud

The Reserve Bank of India released its Payments Vision 2028 policy roadmap themed "Shaping India's Payment Frontier." The most notable provision introduces shared liability between sender's bank and receiver's bank for unauthorised digital transactions — a first for India — aiming to boost consumer trust in digital payments. E-commerce marketplaces and payment aggregators will come directly under RBI regulatory oversight.

  • Theme: "Shaping India's Payment Frontier" (beyond the earlier "4Es" framework)
  • PaSS (Payments Switching Service): Allows seamless migration of EMIs, standing instructions when switching banks
  • Universal Switch On/Off: Expanded from cards to all digital payment methods
  • RBI is established under RBI Act, 1934 | Payment & Settlement Systems Act, 2007 governs digital payments
  • UPI processes over 18 billion transactions monthly (2026 estimate)
CLAT Angle: RBI's regulatory powers, consumer protection in digital transactions, Payment and Settlement Systems Act, and cybersecurity in fintech are increasingly tested in CLAT GK passages.
PUBLIC HEALTH · 24 MARCH

World TB Day 2026: India Launches 100-Day Campaign; 21% Incidence Reduction

On World TB Day (March 24), the President of India highlighted progress under the TB Mukt Bharat Abhiyan — noting a 21% reduction in TB incidence since 2015, nearly twice the global average rate of decline. India launched a new 100-day intensified campaign using AI-based diagnostics to detect asymptomatic TB. Over 20 crore people have been screened, leading to detection of over 32 lakh cases.

  • World TB Day: Observed annually on March 24 to mark day Koch announced TB discovery (1882)
  • Ni-kshay Mitra: Individuals/institutions adopt TB patients for nutritional and vocational support
  • AI deployment: Over 3,000 AI-powered handheld X-ray units (Ni-kshay Vahans)
  • India's target: TB elimination by 2025 (revised to 2027) — ahead of global target of 2030
  • 50% of TB patients in India are asymptomatic — making active surveillance critical
CLAT Angle: Important Days (TB Day — March 24), health schemes, Right to Health under Article 21, and India's progress on SDG 3 (Good Health) are frequently tested in CLAT GK passages.

International Affairs

WEST ASIA CRISIS · CRITICAL

🔴 Crude Oil Crosses $116/Barrel; PM Modi Addresses Parliament on West Asia Crisis

PM Modi addressed the Lok Sabha during the Budget Session on the escalating West Asia conflict (now in its 4th week), outlining five key diplomatic signals. India imports over 88% of its crude oil, making energy security the most urgent domestic concern. The Centre reviewed fuel supply chains and held a virtual meeting with Chief Ministers on inflation management. Russia also announced a ban on gasoline exports from April 1, citing the West Asia situation — putting further pressure on global energy markets.

  • India's response: Strategic autonomy — not siding with any bloc; calling for dialogue
  • India has ~9.4 million diaspora in Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries — largest in the world
  • Strait of Hormuz: ~20% of global crude oil passes through this chokepoint
  • India's Strategic Petroleum Reserves (SPRs): Located in Visakhapatnam, Mangalore & Padur — can cover ~9.5 days of crude imports
CLAT Angle: India's foreign policy doctrine of strategic autonomy, energy security, and the Indian diaspora's rights overseas connect to Constitutional and international law topics in CLAT.

🏆 Bhubaneswar to Host 2028 World Athletics Indoor Championships

India was awarded hosting rights for the 2028 World Athletics Indoor Championships, with Bhubaneswar, Odisha selected as the host city. This makes India the fourth Asian nation to host the event. Bhubaneswar is already home to the Kalinga Stadium, one of India's premier sports infrastructure hubs.

CLAT Angle: India's sports governance, hosting of international events, and Articles 51A(g) relating to excellence in national life are occasionally tested.

🌾 Food Security Index 2026: Iceland Tops; India Among Most Vulnerable Large Economies

Iceland secured the top position in the Food Security Index 2026. India, despite being a leading agricultural economy, was ranked among the most vulnerable large economies in food security with a baseline score of 5.31 against a global average of 6.74. This reflects persistent challenges in food affordability, availability, and quality — especially in rural areas.

CLAT Angle: International indexes (Food Security, Hunger, Human Development, Press Freedom) are a reliable GK topic in CLAT. The Right to Food as part of Article 21 and DPSP Article 47 are also key constitutional angles.

Environment, Science & Technology

BIODIVERSITY · NORTHEAST INDIA

New Butterfly Species Euthalia zubeengargi Discovered in Arunachal Pradesh

Scientists announced the discovery of a new butterfly species — Euthalia zubeengargi — in Arunachal Pradesh, named after popular Assamese singer Zubeen Garg. The discovery underscores the rich biodiversity of Northeast India's Eastern Himalayan corridor, one of the world's 36 recognised biodiversity hotspots.

  • Arunachal Pradesh is in the Indo-Burma Biodiversity Hotspot
  • India is a megadiverse country — one of 17 globally
  • Biological Diversity Act, 2002: Governs access to biological resources and benefit-sharing
  • Wildlife Protection Act, 1972: Basis for protection of fauna in India
CLAT Angle: Biodiversity hotspots, Biological Diversity Act, and India's obligations under the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD) are environment law topics tested in CLAT.

PM E-DRIVE Scheme Extended till March 2028

The PM Electric Drive Revolution in Innovative Vehicle Enhancement (PM E-DRIVE) scheme has been extended till March 31, 2028. 🎯 Weekly CLAT Practice Quiz

Attempt first — then click "Reveal Answer" to check.

Q1. In the landmark March 2026 judgment on Permanent Commission for women officers, which article did the Supreme Court invoke to find systemic discrimination in the Armed Forces?
Articles 14 (equality before law) and 21 (right to dignity and personal liberty). The Court in Lt. Col. Pooja Pal v. Union of India (2026 INSC 281) held that arbitrary and non-transparent selection methods for Permanent Commission denied equal opportunity and violated women officers' dignity. Article 16 (equal opportunity in public employment) is also relevant.
Q2. India signed the UN Convention Against Torture (UNCAT) in 1997 but has not ratified it. What is the legal significance of the distinction between "signing" and "ratifying" an international treaty?
Signing a treaty signals a state's intention to be bound but creates no binding legal obligation. Ratification is the formal acceptance of a treaty's terms into domestic law, making the obligations legally binding under international law. India's failure to ratify UNCAT means it is not legally bound by its anti-torture provisions under international law, and there is currently no domestic law defining or penalising torture.
Q3. The Noida International Airport (Jewar) was built under a PPP model. Flughafen Zürich AG won the bid to build and operate it for how many years? And which company was awarded the civil construction contract?
Flughafen Zürich AG (operator of Zurich Airport, Switzerland) won the bid to build and operate the airport for 40 years. The civil construction contract was awarded to Tata Projects Limited in June 2022. The airport's IATA code is DXN.
Q4. Under the Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order 1950, which three religious communities are eligible to be notified as Scheduled Castes?
Hindus (original, 1950), Sikhs (added in 1956), and Buddhists (added in 1990). Christians and Muslims are excluded. The Ranganath Misra Commission (2007) had recommended extending SC status to Dalit Christians and Muslims, but the recommendation has not been implemented. This is currently a sub-judice matter before the Supreme Court.
Q5. World TB Day is observed on March 24 each year. What event does it commemorate, and what is India's national TB elimination target year (as revised)?
World TB Day commemorates March 24, 1882, when Dr. Robert Koch announced his discovery of Mycobacterium tuberculosis, the bacterium that causes TB. India's revised national target is to eliminate TB by 2027 — five years ahead of the global WHO target of 2030. The programme is called TB Mukt Bharat Abhiyan.
